About the Collaboration
Artist Bio

The collaboration for this book actually happened a long time ago and includes much more than this tangible manifestation. Britt is one of my closest friends. Several months back when I handed my entire platinum process book over to her I gave her no direction or path of how I wanted the final product to appear. It was in her hands to parse what she knows about me and disassemble, cut up, repurpose and remaster the book to create something that reflects both of us. The end result, without a doubt, is the culmination of years of long conversations, innumerable walks, frequent listening sessions, and discussions about you-name-it. This book is a rare representation of what happens when you make amazing connections with people in life.


About the process book:
In 2003 I began to perfect my platinum printing techniques. This involved long, laborious days in the darkroom mixing different amounts of platinum and palladium, hand coating that mixture onto different types of paper and exposing those in a UV light box. The testing started out as step tests that measured how the platinum metal ratio, paper choice and time combination rendered the tones between white and black. After the step tests were done I moved on to testing different areas of my actual prints. I kept a large notebook of every paper test, ratio test and time test I made. After all the testing was completed I printed the final pieces with accuracy and little waste. I also ended up with a very technical process journal full of chemistry notes, repetitive and scientific-looking scribbles and strips and strips of variations of the same image.

b. 1976
Jackson, TN
Amanda McCadams received her MFA in photography in 2006 from the University of Georgia, Athens and her B.S. in photography in 1999 from Middle Tennessee State University. Up until 2012 Amanda worked as a professor and department chair of photography; recently she expanded her artistic toolset and currently works as an interactive designer creating work using new technology, photography and code. She is based in Nashville, TN.
