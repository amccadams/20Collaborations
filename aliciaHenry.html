Artist Bio

Themes of isolation and interaction are recurring in my work. I am interested in the complexities surrounding relationships as well as societal differences and how these concepts shape and influence behavior. 20 Collaborations in Book Arts The goal of the project was to collaborate, design, and complete books in unique, challenging, and interesting ways. Chicago, IL
Alicia Henry received her B.F.A. from the School of the Art Institute of Chicago, and her M. F.A. from Yale University. Henry has received numerous grants, residency, and awards, for example, Skowhegan School of Painting and Sculpture, the Ford Foundation, the John Simon Guggenheim Foundation, Joan Mitchell Foundation, Art in General, MacDowell Art Colony and the Fine Arts Work Center in Provincetown to mention a few. Henry’s works have been exhibited nationally and internationally and are held in private and public collections. She has exhibited regionally at Fisk University’s Van Vechten and Aaron Douglas Galleries, Zeitgeist Gallery, Cheekwood Museum, the Frist Center for the Visual Arts, and Rhodes College’s Clough-Hanson Gallery among others institutions. Henry is an Associate Professor in the Language and Arts Department at Fisk University.
