Lain York

Stock Characters/Dialogue Boxes

Cut vinyl
About the Collaboration
Artist Bio

These images on hand-cut adhesive vinyl are influenced by political cartoons/caricatures from the 18th and 19th century. They convey a sense of the political climate at that time and I like the idea that many of the characters and narratives, despite fashion and particular signifiers, seemed relevant to any time period. The primary interest here, though, is shape, color, texture and how that relates to abstract painting.
It is certainly an honor to be invited to participate with artists of this caliber. I feel this project is especially important because it was initiated by an artist, conceived of as an extension of the local studio network, and produced independently of commercial galleries, academia, and the museum system. Projects like this go a long way in articulating what motivates studio artists and the language they use when left to “discuss” their work among themselves.

b. 1965
Lebanon, TN
Nashville-based artist Lain York has exhibited in Tennessee at Cheekwood Museum of Art; Leu Gallery, Belmont University; the University of the South, Sewanee; and Zeitgeist Gallery; and elsewhere at Bolm Projects, Austin, Texas; Flood Projects, Asheville, NC; Tulca Arts Festival, Galway, Ireland; the University of Alabama Huntsville; and the University of Arkansas, Fayetteville. The Frist Center for the Visual Arts will present his work in the Gordon Contemporary Artists Project Gallery space in January 2014. His artwork is in the collections of FirstBank Tennessee/Ayers Collection of Community; EMI Music, Los Angeles; St. Andrew's- Sewanee School, Sewanee; The Savannah College of Art and Design; Tennessee State Arts Commission; Tennessee State Museum; The Metropolitan Nashville Arts Commission; and Trans-Financial Bank, Nashville. York earned the Bachelor of Arts degree from the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, in 1990. He is represented by Zeitgeist Gallery, where he works as gallery director.
