Rachel Kirk

Vespula

Acrylic, graphite
About the Collaboration
Artist Bio

For the 20 Collaborations in Book Art show, Rachel created a two-sided accordion style book entitled Vespula . It takes the viewer on a step-by-step journey through the process of visual abstraction. The imagery that begins as a photorealistic graphite drawing of a yellow jacket (vespula) quickly morphs into colorful fragmented pieces with no resemblance to the source image. As the viewer walks around the book to see the reverse side, the opposite progression happens - photorealism develops from chaos. Like nature itself, Vespula is cyclical and endless.

b. 1978
Nashville, TN
Rachel Kirk earned her MFA in drawing and painting in 2004 from The University of Tennessee, Knoxville. She is an Adjunct Professor of art at O'More College of Design and The Art Institute of Tennessee - Nashville. Her abstract artwork, which she has shown locally and nationally, is based on patterns found in nature and combines elements of both drawing and painting. She currently lives in East Nashville with her husband and two adorable Westies.
