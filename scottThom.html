About the Collaboration
Artist Bio

When Britt asked me to be a part of this project, I very nearly said no, as its been nearly a decade since I've made an artwork. I have a life in the arts, but it involves helping other people realize their artistic visions.   
Making an image appear to move, whether on a screen or in the drum of a zoetrope, takes an enormous amount of effort and resources. This effort is is so well hidden that I sometimes forget the wondrous combination of science, art, and biology each moving image represents.
With nods to Muybridge and arcane machinery, Endless is a mechanical flip book -  with a great deal of effort the image of the bison will keep running as long as the crank is turned.
The bound book, like the bison, is on the verge of obsolescence, its continued survival no longer a matter of simple reproduction, but only due to the dedication of people to keep it alive. Bison, and soon bound books, no longer exist by their own fact, but only because of someone's love.

I received my BFA from the University of Connecticut, and my MFA in printmaking from UT Austin. I currently live in Nashville, TN, where I work in the museum field.
