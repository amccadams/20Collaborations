Artist Bio

“I am unpacking my Library, Yes I am. The books are not yet on the shelves, not yet touched by the mild boredom of order.”
Walter Benjamin, Unpacking My Library, A Talk about Book Collecting
Unpacking My Library, Petite Tigers, consists of nine double-page spreads plus a front piece and a tailpiece. Each collage and printed page remains unbound. This series is derived from an earlier set of montages titled, Allegory: Petite Tigers.
In a catalog accompanying that group exhibition Mark Scala writes:
The traces and imprints of our artifacts and inheritance, whether parlayed through film, music, or, in the collages of Terry Thacker, photographic images, have the capacity to not only define who we are, but also what we have lost.
The ephemerality, altered meanings, and suggestions of loss in Thacker’s harbingers of broad collapse relate them to the idea of the palimpsest. This is the term for a surface on which one text has been scraped off or otherwise removed to make way for another, leaving only ghostly evidence of ideas, meanings, and images for the forensic linguist or archaeologist to decipher.
Unpacking My Library, Petite Tigers, plays with the idea of a book: folding, reading, imprinting, indexing, publishing, quoting, marginal note taking, etc.

b. 1955
Nashville, TN
Terry Thacker is based in Nashville, TN.
