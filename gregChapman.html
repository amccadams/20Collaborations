Artist Bio

Britt asked Greg to write an essay that she could illustrate for this project. Greg recalled an essay he had written years ago anonymously to slip into the pile of final student essays the English teachers were grading in order to lighten their mood a bit. Britt loved it, so it became part of this project. We hope you enjoy it as well.

b. 1960
DeKalb County, AL
Dr. Greg Chapman was born in 1960 in DeKalb County in northeast Alabama. He attended Ider High School in Ider, Alabama, and, after graduating with an A. A. from Northeast Alabama State Junior College and a B.A. in English and Journalism from the University of Alabama in Tuscaloosa, he returned to his home county, where he lived in Rainsville and taught at Ider from 1981 until 1990. He earned an M.A. in English in 1983 and an Ed.D. in Curriculum & Instruction in 1991. He taught at Fort Payne High School from 1990 until 1992 before taking a position as English instructor at Snead State Community College in Boaz, Alabama. In 2002, he moved into administration at Snead State, becoming Director of Distance Learning, Director of Instruction, and, ultimately, Dean of Academic Services. In 2009, he retired from the Alabama System and moved to Nashville to become Dean of Academic Affairs at the Art Institute of Tennessee â€“ Nashville, where he currently works. While there, he hired Britt Stadig to be Academic Director for the General Education, Transitional Studies, and Art Foundations departments. Through a great relationship of mutual respect and admiration, Greg and Britt developed a wonderful friendship, and this collaboration has been a fortunate by-product.
